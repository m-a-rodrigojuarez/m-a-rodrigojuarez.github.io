Case 1 · Walmart

Reducing onboarding friction & improving activation

A mixed-method discovery initiative to understand why new users were dropping off during onboarding and delaying their first task in a last-mile delivery platform launched in Mexico.

01

Context

Walmart launched a global last-mile delivery platform in Mexico, but early product signals showed that new users were struggling to move from registration into their first meaningful task.

02

Problem

The platform showed low activation rates among new users, with significant friction during onboarding and early task completion. Initial signals pointed to high drop-off, delays in first tasks, and misalignment between product flows and real-world operations.

03

Research approach

I led a mixed-method discovery initiative combining qualitative insights, behavioral signals, contextual interviews, competitive flow analysis, and usability testing to identify opportunities for activation improvement.

04

My role

I defined the research strategy and scope, designed and conducted studies, synthesized behavioral patterns, translated insights into product direction, and facilitated cross-functional workshops.

05

Impact

The research informed a Design Sprint and led to a proposed onboarding journey focused on faster activation. The recommendation moved insurance and tax requirements out of the critical path, allowing interested users to start working sooner while still making those requirements available later to unlock larger orders and higher earning potential.

Onboarding journey

1 Account set-up
2 Identity requirements
3 Identity validation
4 Banking requirements
5 Insurance requirements
6 Tax requirements
7 Validation of requirements
8 Activated

Key insight themes

Onboarding overload

Too many decisions early in the journey increased hesitation and drop-off risk.

Lack of clarity

Unclear next steps created confusion during critical activation moments.

Context mismatch

Product flows did not fully align with real-world operational conditions.
