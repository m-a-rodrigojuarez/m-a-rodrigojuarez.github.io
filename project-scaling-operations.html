UX Research Case Study

Scaling operations to support growth

Strategic discovery and market optimization for an on-demand delivery service

We partnered with cross-functional teams to understand operational constraints, cost-to-serve variability, and market dynamics across regions to inform scalable growth decisions.

01

Context

A rapidly growing on-demand delivery service was expanding into new cities. Leadership needed clarity on operational constraints, market variability, and key levers to scale efficiently while preserving service quality.

02

Problem

Growth was outpacing operational readiness in several markets, leading to inconsistent performance, higher cost-to-serve, and missed opportunities in high-potential areas.

03

Research approach

We used a mixed-methods approach to uncover operational bottlenecks, understand regional differences, and identify opportunities to optimize coverage, capacity, and experience.

04

My role

I led the end-to-end research effort, from defining the scope to synthesizing insights and partnering with product, operations, and strategy teams to drive actionable recommendations.

05

Impact

Our insights shaped prioritization, informed go-to-market strategy, and optimized operational investments, helping the company scale smarter and more sustainably.

Operational dependencies

Shared Associate Workload

Store associates managed Local Fleet orders while also supporting 3P vendor operations.

Demand-Mismatch Time Slots

Delivery windows built for high-demand cities increased operating costs in lower-demand markets.

Local Route Adjustments

Stores adapted system routes around local constraints, staffing, and operational reality.

Limited Shopper App Support

The shopper app lacked the order management and real-time communication tools needed to coordinate with customers and store staff.

Key insight themes

Cost-to-Serve Trade-offs

Stores outside high-demand areas had to choose between optimizing cost-to-serve and preserving operational quality.

Cross-functional Growth Model

Scaling OD delivery required synchronized decisions across UX, Operations, and Product to protect service quality.

Expanded User Definition

The mobile app needed to support not only end customers, but also owned and third-party shoppers executing the service.

Strategic impact

Decision-making framework

The journey map and cost analysis were adopted by Product, Operations, and Business stakeholders as a shared guide for decision-making.

Business model redesign

The insights helped the team begin redefining the operating model, planning logic, and execution model for on-demand shopping.

Operational migration reference

The blueprint became a reference artifact for operational changes and the migration from third-party tools to Walmart-owned capabilities.
