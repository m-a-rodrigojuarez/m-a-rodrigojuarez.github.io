Hoteles City Express · Cross-functional Delivery & Incident Response

Leading the launch and hypercare of OpenPay in Colombia

Coordinating cross-functional delivery and incident response to shift two hotels from pay-at-hotel to prepaid bookings

01 · Context

Prepaid booking opportunity

Finance identified an opportunity to shift Colombia hotels from pay-at-hotel to prepaid online bookings through OpenPay, securing revenue before check-in instead of at checkout.

02 · Problem

Operational readiness gap

Launching required coordinating Legal, Tech, Operations, Call Center, and hotel front desks around a new payment flow and live PMS registration errors.

03 · Approach

Delivery and hypercare

I ran pre-launch delivery tracking and led a month-long hypercare period with twice-daily cross-functional sessions and incident logging.

04 · My role

Execution ownership

The business case came from Finance, but product execution was mine: delivery coordination, triage, workarounds, ownership, and backlog definition.

05 · Impact

Stable prepaid process

The launch shifted reservations to prepaid bookings, and hypercare turned live incidents into a stable, permanent operating process.

Delivery model

From launch coordination to live incident response

The work required more than shipping a payment integration. It meant aligning legal readiness, product copy, technical implementation, hotel operations, Call Center processes, and live incident response into one coordinated launch system.

Turning a launch checklist into cross-team delivery

One board, every missing piece

Before launch, I tracked every outstanding item, screens, copy, legal sign-off, tech readiness, on a single delivery board, instead of chasing status across six separate teams.

Coordinating six teams against one deadline

Product, Legal, Finance, Operations, Call Center, and Tech each owned different pieces of the launch. The board made it visible who was blocking what, so I could unblock teams before it threatened the release date.

Turning incident chaos into a repeatable triage system

Categorizing by type, not by team

As incidents came in through front desks and the Call Center, I logged each one on a Trello board and categorized it by type (operational, financial, technical) rather than by who reported it, then assigned an owner to follow through.

Workarounds now, permanent fixes later

When a fix couldn't happen immediately, I coordinated a temporary workaround with the affected teams and pushed the permanent solution into a backlog, so open issues never blocked the hotels' day-to-day operation.
