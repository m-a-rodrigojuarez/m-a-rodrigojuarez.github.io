About me

Product manager, researcher, mentor, and constant learner.

Hi, I'm Rodrigo, a Senior Product Manager & UX Researcher living in Ensenada, Baja California. My background combines computer science, software development, research, and product strategy, grounded in a practical understanding of real-world operations.

I studied Computer Engineering at the Instituto Tecnologico Autonomo de Mexico (ITAM) and later earned a Master's degree in Computer Science from the Centro de Investigacion Cientifica y de Educacion Superior de Ensenada (CICESE), with a specialization in Human-Computer Interaction.

I recently worked at Walmart MX. I started as a Research & UX Ops manager, and then I switched to a UX Research Manager role. In that role, I led research across eCommerce and OmniSupply Transportation platforms, turning customer and business questions into decisions the teams could act on.

Before that, I worked at Sperientia, a UX research studio. I started as a UX Researcher on agile mobile and desktop product teams, delivering complete studies in five business days, and was later promoted to UX Research Co-Lead, where I worked directly with clients to translate business questions into research plans and built a training program for junior researchers.

Before working in research full-time, I spent 5 years at Hoteles City Express, where I moved from Back-end Developer to IT Project Leader to Commercial Products Manager. By the end, I was the one making product decisions directly: defining MVPs, negotiating with Engineering and Finance, and launching new business lines grounded in user and market research.

I currently teach Digital Product Evaluation Methods and direct students' dissertation research at Universidad ICESI, which keeps me close to how the next generation is learning to do this work.

In my free time, I like to play football, do CrossFit, and spend time with my family and friends.

Research Approach

Behavior-driven product research

My work focuses on identifying friction across critical journeys, understanding behavioral patterns, and translating insights into product opportunities I can act on.

Product activation

Understanding onboarding friction, user behavior, and critical moments across digital journeys to improve activation and time-to-value.

Strategic scalability

Connecting operational context, market variability, and business priorities to support scalable product and service decisions.

Behavioral optimization

Using behavioral analysis and flow optimization to identify opportunities that improve conversion and business performance.

Publications

Effect of technology on aging perception

Technology can support active aging, but its impact on aging perception depends on older adults’ prior attitudes toward their own aging process. This study found that technology use can influence perceived aging and led to a sociotechnical model connecting technology use with successful aging.

View publication
Do technology-related stimuli affect age estimation?

The study explored whether exposure to technology-related concepts changes aging perception through age estimation. Results suggested that actual technology use, studied longitudinally with older adults, may be more relevant for understanding how technology influences aging perception.

View publication
Mental models, performance and usability of a complex interactive system: the case of Twitter

This study analyzed how people’s mental models and expertise relate to Twitter usability and performance. Results showed that expertise had a stronger impact on performance, while both expertise and mental model style influenced perceived usability.

View publication
weRemember: Letting AD patients to enjoy their home and their families

weRemember is a prototype that supports people with Alzheimer’s in maintaining independence at home and communicating with family. Iterative evaluations suggested positive effects on caregiver relationships and the home experience.
