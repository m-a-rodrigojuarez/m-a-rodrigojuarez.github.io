UX Research Case Study

Optimizing booking flow through behavior analysis

Behavioral analysis and conversion optimization for a centralized hotel reservation platform

We analyzed booking behaviors and reservation flows to optimize booking processing and connect experience improvements to measurable business growth.

01 · Context

Growing OTA dependency

Hoteles City Express relied on a growing set of OTAs to sell rooms. Each new agency arrived with its own process, rules, and data structure.

02 · Problem

Slow integrations, manual fixes

New OTA integrations took close to two months, and rejected reservations required Call Center agents to work across three systems.

03 · Approach

Shared architecture

I led the Channel Manager redesign around reusable components, centralized inventory and pricing, and agency-specific logic only where needed.

04 · My role

End-to-end product decision

I defined the target architecture, prioritized the redesign, partnered with Engineering, and validated the solution against real booking data.

05 · Impact

Scalable channel growth

The redesign enabled 12 OTAs, GDS integration, hourly bookings, and measurable growth over 12 months.

Architecture blueprint

From isolated integrations to scalable product infrastructure

The redesign transformed the Channel Manager from a collection of isolated OTA flows into a shared product architecture that could scale integrations, automate exceptions, and unlock new distribution models.

Turning fragmented integrations into centralized control

From one-off integrations to a shared architecture

The original architecture treated every OTA as a fully independent entity, so each new integration started from zero. We redesigned it around generic, reusable components for what OTAs had in common, layered with OTA-specific logic only where it was actually needed.

Centralized inventory and pricing control

The same architecture enabled bulk inventory and pricing updates across properties and OTAs from a single screen at headquarters, replacing manual, property-by-property updates.

Handling exceptions at scale

Generic error registration instead of manual fixes

Rejected reservations used to require manual integration, case by case, depending on the OTA. Working with Operations, we prioritized registering them with generic, error-type-based information so each hotel could confirm the details at check-in.

Self-service reprocessing for rate and availability errors

For rejections caused by unavailable rates or room types, we built a module that surfaced the error directly to hotels. Once inventory was opened or the missing rate was loaded, the reservation reprocessed automatically.

From a 5-9 minute manual process to one click

Before: a five-step manual fix across three systems

Call Center agents had to open the OTA portal, review the error, switch to the City Express PMS, enable missing rate or inventory, manually register the reservation, and return to the OTA portal to confirm it.

After: one click inside the Channel Manager

We built a screen showing every reservation with an error. Each one had a reprocess button that triggered automated PMS registration and OTA confirmation, without manually entering either system.

Turning architecture into new business lines

Enabling hourly bookings for airport hotels

Working with Business and Finance, we found that airport-hotel guests often needed a short stay to shower and rest. We adapted the architecture to support hourly bookings through ByHours, including its own pricing and inventory logic.

Enabling GDS agreements and surfacing new direct deals

We adapted the flow to support GDS-based corporate agreement rates and built a report identifying which companies generated the most GDS reservations, helping Operations approach them directly.
