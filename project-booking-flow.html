UX Research Case Study

Optimizing booking flow through behavior analysis

Behavioral analysis and conversion optimization for a centralized hotel reservation platform

We analyzed booking behaviors and reservation flows to optimize booking processing and connect experience improvements to measurable business growth.

01

Context

Large hotel chains depend on multiple sales channels, and online travel agencies are among the most critical in the digital ecosystem. Each agency operates with its own rules, data structures, and booking flows, making operations more complex with every new integration.

02

Problem

The centralized platform was handling agency flows as isolated processes, without clear automation rules or a deep understanding of real usage and processing patterns. This created high operational costs, frequent reservation errors, and lost bookings that directly affected revenue.

03

Research approach

I led a historical analysis of reservation behavior with operational teams, reviewed automated booking flows, and partnered with development teams to identify recurring patterns, inefficiencies, and opportunities for optimization.

04

My role

I led reservation behavior analysis, conducted automated flow evaluations, reviewed operational workflows, collaborated with development teams during implementation, and defined optimization opportunities.

05

Impact

Our insights informed standardized workflows grounded in actual user and processing patterns. This increased the number of reservations processed correctly and simplified the integration of new online travel agencies.

Key findings

Behavior varies by flow structure

Reservation behavior varied significantly depending on flow structure and operational dependencies.

Automation introduced friction

Automated flows introduced friction during critical booking decision-making moments.

Process and logic were misaligned

Misalignment between operational processes and booking logic affected efficiency and user progression.

Key insight themes

Decision overload

Users struggled when comparison criteria and booking options were not clear enough.

Trust and clarity gaps

Uncertainty around rates, policies, and next steps slowed reservation completion.

Checkout friction

Form and payment steps introduced avoidable hesitation near conversion.
